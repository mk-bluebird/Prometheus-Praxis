Field: shardinstance.evidencehex

Definition:
  evidencehex is a hex-encoded content hash over the canonical shard payload.
  The payload consists of:
    1. The ALN header and schema row (particle metadata).
    2. The CSV body rows sorted by primary index (e.g., time, nodeid).
    3. Normalized line endings (LF) and UTF-8 encoding.

Hashing algorithm:
  - Algorithm: SHA-256
  - Input: canonical byte sequence of the shard payload
  - Output: 32-byte digest, encoded as 64 lowercase hex characters

Usage:
  - On emission, the producer computes SHA-256 over the canonicalized payload
    and stores the resulting hex string in shardinstance.evidencehex.
  - Consumers and agents re-compute the hash over the local payload and
    compare it against evidencehex. Any mismatch indicates tampering
    or non-canonical formatting and must mark the shard as non-deployable.
